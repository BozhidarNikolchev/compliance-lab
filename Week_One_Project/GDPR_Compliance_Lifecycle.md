## 📌 Introduction
The GDPR Compliance Lifecycle is a **continuous and structured operational framework** that organizations must follow to ensure personal data is processed lawfully, transparently, and securely.  
This document outlines the six stages used in real-world compliance governance.

---

## 📚 Scope & Assumptions
This lifecycle applies to:
- All systems processing personal data  
- All employees and contractors  
- All third-party processors  

Assumptions:
- The organization operates within or processes data of EU residents  
- The organization processes personal data in digital systems  

---

# 1. Data Mapping & Discovery
### Objective  
Identify what personal data exists, where it flows, and how it is processed.

### Includes:
- Data inventory  
- Data flow diagrams  
- Legal basis identification  
- Storage location analysis  

### Evidence Examples:
- Updated RoPA  
- Data flow charts  
- System inventory sheets  

---

# 2. Gap Assessment
### Objective  
Detect non‑compliance issues and evaluate existing controls.

### Includes:
- Missing policies  
- Incorrect or unclear legal basis  
- Over‑collection of data  
- Non‑compliant third‑party processors  

### Evidence Examples:
- Gap analysis report  
- Risk register entries  

---

# 3. Remediation & Controls Implementation
### Objective  
Deploy the required controls to fix identified issues.

### Controls:
- Organizational: policies, guidelines, training  
- Technical: MFA, encryption, logging  
- Procedural: DSAR workflows, retention processes  
- Contractual: DPA updates  

### Evidence Examples:
- Approved policies  
- System configuration screenshots  
- Vendor assessment forms  

---

# 4. Monitoring & Evidence Collection
### Objective  
Ensure GDPR controls work effectively over time.

### Includes:
- Access reviews  
- Audit log monitoring  
- Vendor risk monitoring  
- Training compliance tracking  

### Evidence Examples:
- Audit trails  
- Review reports  
- Compliance dashboards  

---

# 5. Incident Response & Breach Management
### Objective  
Detect, assess, and respond to personal data breaches.

### Workflow:
1. Detection  
2. Containment  
3. Impact analysis  
4. 72‑hour regulatory notification (if required)  
5. Communication to data subjects (if high risk)  
6. Post‑incident remediation  

### Evidence Examples:
- Incident report  
- Root cause analysis  
- Corrective action document  

---

# 6. Continuous Improvement
### Objective  
Maintain long‑term compliance readiness.

### Includes:
- Annual compliance reviews  
- Policy updates  
- Vendor reassessments  
- Risk reassessments  

### Evidence Examples:
- Annual audit report  
- Updated RoPA  
- Updated policies  

---

## 🧾 Audit‑Ready Summary
The GDPR lifecycle ensures:
- Ongoing compliance  
- Accountability  
- Proper governance structure  
- Clear data protection responsibilities
