# Interview Answer — Explain the GDPR Compliance Lifecycle

The GDPR compliance lifecycle is a **continuous, structured process** organizations follow to ensure that personal data is collected, stored, processed, and protected lawfully.

---

## 📘 The Six Stages
1. **Data Mapping & Discovery**  
   Example: Identifying where customer data is stored in internal systems.  

2. **Gap Assessment**  
   Example: Finding missing policies or inadequate retention rules.  

3. **Remediation & Controls**  
   Example: Implementing MFA and updating the Privacy Notice.  

4. **Monitoring & Evidence**  
   Example: Tracking logs and performing quarterly access reviews.  

5. **Incident Response & Breach Management**  
   Example: Reporting a breach to authorities within 72 hours.  

6. **Continuous Improvement**  
   Example: Updating RoPA annually and reassessing third‑party vendors.  

---

## 🎯 Why It Exists
Because GDPR requires **ongoing accountability**, not one‑time compliance.  
The lifecycle ensures transparency, security, and continuous adaptation to risks.
