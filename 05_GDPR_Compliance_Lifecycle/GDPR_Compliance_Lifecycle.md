# GDPR Compliance Lifecycle — Documentation
**Author:** Bozhidar Nikolchev 
**Role:** Compliance Analyst Trainee  

---

## 1. Data Mapping & Discovery
- Identify and classify personal data.
- Map data flows across systems.
- Document where personal data is stored.
- Identify legal basis and access rights.
- Maintain an accurate RoPA.

---

## 2. Gap Assessment
- Evaluate the current state vs GDPR principles.
- Identify missing policies or unclear legal bases.
- Detect high-risk or excessive data processing.
- Document compliance gaps across teams.

---

## 3. Remediation & Controls Implementation
- Implement missing GDPR policies.
- Deploy technical security controls (MFA, encryption, logs).
- Establish DSAR and breach response workflows.
- Review and update Data Processing Agreements (DPAs).

---

## 4. Monitoring & Evidence Collection
- Maintain compliance logs.
- Keep detailed audit evidence.
- Review user access regularly.
- Track employee GDPR training and vendor compliance.

---

## 5. Incident Response & Breach Management
- Detect and classify personal data breaches.
- Perform risk and impact assessment.
- Notify the authorities within 72 hours (if required).
- Implement corrective controls and post‑incident improvements.

---

## 6. Continuous Improvement
- Annual risk assessments.
- Policy reviews and RoPA updates.
- Vendor reassessments.
- Ongoing compliance training.

---

## Summary
The GDPR lifecycle is a continuous loop requiring organizations to protect, monitor, update, and demonstrate responsible handling of personal data.
