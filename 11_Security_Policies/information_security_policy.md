Version: 1.0  
Status: Draft  
Last Updated: 2026‑03‑22  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to establish the organization’s security principles and management direction to protect the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy of information assets. This policy defines responsibilities and provides a governance structure for maintaining an effective Information Security Management Framework (ISMF).

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party personnel  
- All systems, networks, cloud environments, and applications  
- All physical and digital information assets  
- All organizational locations and remote environments  

---

## 3. Policy Statement
The organization shall implement and maintain an information security framework aligned to internationally recognized standards and regulatory requirements. The organization is committed to:

- Protecting information assets from unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, loss, or destruction  
- Maintaining and enforcing all security policies  
- Performing regular risk assessments and applying risk-based controls  
- Ensuring continuous monitoring and detection of security events  
- Providing mandatory security awareness training  
- Meeting all legal, contractual, and compliance requirements  

---

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### Executive Management
- Approves the policy and allocates required resources  
- Ensures alignment with organizational objectives and compliance obligations  

### Information Security Officer / GRC Lead
- Maintains the Information Security Management Framework (ISMF)  
- Oversees risk management, policies, audits, and compliance activities  
- Coordinates incident response and security governance  

### IT Department
- Implements and maintains security controls  
- Manages user access, monitoring systems, and infrastructure protections  

### Employees and Contractors
- Must read, understand, and comply with all security policies  
- Report incidents, suspicious activities, or potential weaknesses immediately  

---

## 5. Information Security Controls

### 5.1 Governance and Policies
- A structured set of security policies must be maintained and reviewed annually  
- Policies shall reflect ISO 27001, NIST CSF, SOC 2, PCI‑DSS, GDPR, and other frameworks  

### 5.2 Risk Management
- The organization must maintain a documented risk management process  
- Risks shall be assessed and treated based on impact and likelihood  

### 5.3 Access Control
- Access must follow least privilege and need-to-know principles  
- MFA shall be enforced for all critical systems  
- Periodic access reviews must be performed and documented  

### 5.4 Asset Management
- All information assets must be inventoried and classified  
- Asset owners must be assigned for each asset  

### 5.5 Physical and Environmental Security
- Access to facilities must be controlled, monitored, and logged  
- Visitors must be authorized and escorted  

### 5.6 Incident Response
- All personnel must report incidents immediately  
- A documented Incident Response Plan (IRP) must be tested regularly  

### 5.7 Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery
- Backup and recovery procedures must exist and be tested  
- Critical systems must have recovery time and recovery point objectives defined

## 7. Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, including termination, legal action, or third‑party contract termination.

---

## 8. Review and Updates
This policy must be reviewed:

- At least annually  
- After major organizational or technological changes  
- Following significant security incidents  
- When legal, regulatory, or framework requirements change
