# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
Author: Bozhidar Nikolchev  
Version: 1.0  
Status: Draft  
Last Updated: 2026‑03‑22  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to define acceptable and responsible use of organizational information systems, networks, devices, and data. This policy ensures that all users understand their responsibilities when accessing organizational assets to protect them from misuse, security incidents, and legal exposure.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third‐party personnel  
- All organizational systems, applications, networks, and cloud services  
- All devices used to access organizational resources, including personal devices (BYOD) when authorized  
- All data processed, stored, or transmitted by the organization   

---

## 3. Acceptable Use Requirements

### 3.1 General Usage
- Systems must be used for authorized business purposes only.  
- Users must comply with all organizational security policies and procedures.  
- Personal use is permitted only when it does not interfere with business operations, violate laws, or introduce risk.

### 3.2 Access and Authentication
- Users must protect their login credentials and never share passwords.  
- Multi‑Factor Authentication (MFA) must be used where required.  
- Access may only be used by the assigned individual — no shared accounts unless explicitly approved.

### 3.3 Data Protection
- Sensitive or confidential data must not be stored on unauthorized devices.  
- Data may only be transmitted using approved communication channels.  
- Users must follow data handling, retention, and disposal procedures.

### 3.4 Network and Device Usage
- Users must not install unauthorized software or security tools.  
- Users must not attempt to bypass security controls (VPN, firewall, proxies).  
- Personal devices must meet organizational security requirements if accessing corporate systems (e.g., encryption, up‐to‐date patches).

### 3.5 Prohibited Activities
The following actions are strictly prohibited:

- Unauthorized access attempts or scanning of systems  
- Using systems for illegal activities or copyright violations  
- Circumventing or disabling security controls  
- Introducing malware or high‑risk software  
- Sharing confidential information with unauthorized parties  
- Accessing inappropriate or harmful content  

---

## 4. Reporting Requirements
- Users must immediately report suspected security incidents, phishing attempts, unauthorized access, lost/stolen devices, or policy violations.  
- Reports must be made to the Security/GRC team using approved channels.

---

## 5. Monitoring and Privacy
- The organization reserves the right to monitor, log, and review all activities conducted on its systems.  
- Users should not expect privacy when using organizational devices or networks.  
- Monitoring is conducted in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

---

## 7. Enforcement
Violations may result in disciplinary action, including:

- Revocation of system access  
- HR disciplinary procedures  
- Contract termination  
- Legal action  

---

## 8. Review and Updates
This policy must be reviewed:

- At least annually  
- After major changes to systems or organizational structure  
- Following significant security incidents  
- When legal or regulatory requirements change
