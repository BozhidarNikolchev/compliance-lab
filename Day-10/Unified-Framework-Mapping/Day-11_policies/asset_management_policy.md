# Asset Management Policy
Author: Bozhidar Nikolchev  
Version: 1.0  
Status: Draft  
Last Updated: 2026‑03‑22  

---

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Asset Management Policy is to ensure all organizational information assets are identified, classified, owned, maintained, and protected throughout their lifecycle. This policy establishes a structured framework for tracking, managing, and securing assets to reduce risks associated with loss, misuse, or unauthorized access.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:

- All organizational information assets (hardware, software, data, cloud services, accounts)  
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties who access or manage assets  
- All environments (on‑premises, cloud, hybrid, remote, mobile)  
- All stages of the asset lifecycle: acquisition, use, maintenance, retirement, and disposal  

---

## 3. Asset Classification and Ownership

### 3.1 Identification
- All information assets must be documented in an official asset inventory.  
- The inventory must include asset type, location, technical owner, business owner, criticality, and classification.

### 3.2 Classification
Assets must be classified according to sensitivity and business impact:

- **Public** – Information intended for public access  
- **Internal** – General internal use only  
- **Confidential** – Sensitive business information  
- **Restricted** – Highly sensitive data requiring the strongest controls  

### 3.3 Ownership
- Every asset must have a designated **business owner** responsible for its proper use.  
- Technical owners manage maintenance, security configuration, and lifecycle.

---

## 4. Acceptable Use of Assets
- Assets may only be used for authorized business purposes.  
- Changes to asset configuration must be approved and documented.  
- Unauthorized software installation or modification is prohibited.  

---

## 5. Asset Lifecycle Management

### 5.1 Acquisition
- Newly acquired assets must be registered in the asset inventory before use.

### 5.2 Maintenance
- Assets must be patched, updated, and maintained according to IT security standards.  
- Vulnerability management must be applied where relevant.

### 5.3 Transfer or Reassignment
- Assets must be updated in the inventory when moved or reassigned.  
- Sensitive data must be cleared before transfer.

### 5.4 Retirement and Disposal
- Assets containing sensitive data must be securely wiped or destroyed.  
- Disposal activities must follow approved destruction methods and be logged.

---

## 6. Cloud and Software Assets

### 6.1 SaaS, IaaS, and Cloud Services
- All cloud services must be approved before use.  
- Cloud assets must be included in the official inventory.  
- Access and configuration must follow organizational security standards.

### 6.2 Software Licensing
- Software must be used in accordance with licensing agreements.  
- Unauthorized or pirated software is prohibited.

---

## 7. Monitoring, Logging, and Audits
- Asset inventories must be reviewed periodically for accuracy.  
- Changes must be logged and traceable.  
- Audits must confirm asset ownership, classification, and security posture.

## 9. Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in:

- Revocation of asset access  
- HR disciplinary action  
- Termination of contract  
- Legal or regulatory consequences  

---

## 10. Review and Updates
This policy must be reviewed:

- At least annually  
- When new asset types or technologies are introduced  
- After incidents or changes affecting asset security  
- When legal or regulatory requirements change
